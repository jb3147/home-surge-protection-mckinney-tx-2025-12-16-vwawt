Frequently Asked Questions About Home Surge Protection

Still have questions about protecting your McKinney home? Here are answers to the most common things homeowners ask us before installing whole-home surge protection.

Whole-home surge protection uses a permanently installed device at your main electrical panel to intercept and divert excess voltage before it spreads through your wiring. It protects every circuit, outlet, and hard‑wired appliance in your home, including items that are not plugged into surge strips. Traditional plug‑in power strips only protect the specific devices connected to them and often provide limited protection that degrades quickly over time. Our systems are industrial‑grade, respond faster, handle larger surges, and create a powerful first line of defense for your entire home rather than isolated spots.

Yes. Many damaging surges never cause a complete power outage. They show up as quick spikes, dips, or flickers caused by lightning nearby, utility switching on the grid, large motors starting up, or issues in neighboring buildings. These transient events can slowly weaken sensitive electronics and modern appliance boards, leading to unexplained failures months later. By installing surge protection at your panel, you smooth out these frequent, smaller disturbances as well as larger, more visible events—reducing stress on your equipment every single day, not just during big storms.

Most installations in McKinney take about 60–90 minutes from arrival to final testing, depending on your panel configuration and access. For safety, we will temporarily shut off power to your home while we connect the surge protective device and verify proper grounding and bonding. This outage is brief and scheduled at a convenient time for you. Once complete, we restore power, test the system, and show you how to verify that your home surge protection is active and operating correctly.

Whole-home surge protection dramatically reduces the energy of surges entering your home, providing broad coverage for all circuits and appliances. For most households, this level of protection is a major upgrade over relying on basic strips alone. That said, for very sensitive or critical equipment—like high‑end home theater systems, servers, or medical devices—we still recommend using quality plug‑in surge strips or uninterruptible power supplies (UPS) as a secondary layer. Think of the panel‑mounted device as your main shield and individual surge strips as extra armor for your most valuable gear.

Pricing depends on your panel configuration and any additional protection needs, but for most McKinney homes, professional installation of whole-home surge protection typically costs less than replacing a single high‑end TV or major appliance. When you consider the total value of what’s plugged into your home—electronics, HVAC systems, kitchen appliances, networking gear—the investment is small compared to the potential cost and inconvenience of surge‑related failures.
